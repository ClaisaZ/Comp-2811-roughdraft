- Color 
-----
- Additive and Subtractive Colors

- Subtractive
    - Start with white
    - White is every color in the spectrum
    - A piece of white paper reflects every color in the spectrum
    - If you draw something on the page (e.g. red marker) you are subtracting/removing 
    - some of the spectrum
    
    e.g. combining primary colors in the subtractive model
    yellow + blue = green

Additive (pixels)
    Start with black
    Red Green Blue are primary colors
        Secondary colors are 
            C(yan)
            M(agenta)
            Y(ellow)
      (blac)K

Color Harmonies
    For your high fidelity wireframe you are required to:
        - provide a three-color color scheme
            primary     most used
            secondary   less used but still prominent
            highlight   sparingly used contrast color 
        - the three color scheme CANNOT include white, black (but you can and should use a lot of white, and probably black)
        - provide the color harmony you used
            - you cannot use analogous or monochromatic
        - pick two fonts
            - one heading font
            - one body font
        - IMPORTANT: your color scheme must validate at least AA for contrast
            use https://webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker/ to test your foreground and background colors
        - IMPORTANT: DO NOT use color to indicate state - color blind people will not understand, so use something in addition to color to communicate (icons, text)

Color and Branding 
    - choose a color that conveys something to your users
    - targeted towards your intended audience


Typography
----------
    Fonts
        font-family -> Typeface (Helvetica Narrow)

    Display Fonts (Headings) - can be more expressive, playful
    
    Body Fonts (body text) - not expressive or playful, focused on readability

    Try to choose two fonts that have a similar feel
        Common practice is to pair a serif and a similar sans-serif

    NO COMIC SANS, NO PAPYRUS/CURSIVE please
